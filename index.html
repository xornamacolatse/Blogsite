Floods: NPA to undertake compliance inspections at affected fuel stations nationwide.

In view of the floods that occurred in Accra and parts of the country, as well as the ongoing rainfall, the National Petroleum Authority (NPA) has told members of the public to avoid entering, driving through, or gathering around flooded fuel stations, and refrain from coming into contact with floodwater in and around affected stations, as it may contain petroleum products or other hazardous contaminants.

By TV3 Media General  ·  News Website ·  1 min read

The NPA also said that the public should immediately report any fuel leaks, unusual petroleum odours, visible fuel sheens on floodwater, or other unsafe conditions to the Ghana National Fire Service or the nearest NPA office, and also comply with all safety instructions issued by emergency responders and regulatory authorities. The NPA also said that fuel station operators shall take all necessary measures to prevent contaminated water, petroleum products, or hazardous substances from entering drains, streams, rivers, or other water bodies.
